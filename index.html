Top 5 AI ETFs

1

METV The METV ETF, known as the Round Hill Ball Metaverse ETF, aims to provide investors with a way to tap into the Metaverse. It seeks to mirror the performance of the Ball Metaverse Index before deducting fees and expenses.

METV can provide actual exposure to companies developing infrastructure essential to Metaverse, such as Cloudflare and NVIDIA. It also exposes the gaming engines responsible for creating a gaming world, such as Unity and Roblox.

2

AIQ The Global X Artificial Intelligence and Technology ETF invests in companies that stand to benefit from the development and utilization of AI in their products, as well as companies that facilitate the use of AI to bring big data.

The AIQ ETF, or Global X Artificial Intelligence & Technology ETF, strives to mirror the price and yield performance of the Index Artificial Intelligence & Big Data Index, excluding fees and expenses.

3

ROBT uses consumer technology to select the companies involved in Artificial Intelligence or robotics. Then, the stocks will be ranked based on their level of involvement in the industry.

The stocks are mainly categorized into one of three categories: enhancers, engagers, and enablers. Here, the engagers got 60% weight, enablers have 25%, and 15% for enhancers. The index has been rebalanced quarterly and reconstituted semi-annually

4

TECB This multisector ETF seeks the investment results of an index composed of US companies that could benefit from technologies such as AI and robotics, cybersecurity, cloud and data tech, and financial technology.

TECB always seeks long-term growth by accessing the companies at the forefront of technology. There is a precise exposure to US stocks and the value chain of five themes, which believes in breakthroughs in the tech industry.

5

PNQI PNQI is mainly designed to track the performance of the companies engaged in Internet-related businesses related to the New York Stock Exchange. NYSE American, and NASDAQ Stock Market.

The fund will typically invest 90% of its total assets in the index's securities. The companies in this index are related to Internet service but not limited to Internet software, search engines, web hosting, or website design.
